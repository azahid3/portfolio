my story

IT & cybersecurity

I started my career at the intersection of IT and cybersecurity. At George Mason University, I became fascinated by how technology rapidly changes, secures, and optimizes businesses. My first real-world experience came at Navy Federal Credit Union, where I managed IT governance and remediated vulnerabilities across the EUC environment, maintaining infrastructure for thousands of employees.

the pivot to growth

While technical security was meaningful, I realized I wanted to understand how products actually reach and engage users. This led me to Institute for Defense Analyses, where I supported the cyber team, securing virtualization infrastructure and workstations.

The real turning point came when I started Khaana as a Growth & Marketing Lead. I learned that growth isn't just marketing—it's engineering. I build acquisition loops, scale distribution on social media, and discover what drives user retention in B2C spaces. This experience fundamentally changed how I approach problems: not as siloed engineering tasks, but as interconnected systems.

In today's landscape, where AI enables products to come to life in days and weeks, the sense of taste in product matters more than ever. I want to continue diversifying my skills and presenting my taste in building products that resonate.

solutions at scale

Today, I work as a Solutions Engineer at Arrow Electronics, supporting enterprises with Nutanix hybrid cloud and edge computing solutions. I bridge the gap between technical architects and business stakeholders, translating complex infrastructure challenges into actionable solutions.

From meeting with execs to conducting demos to understanding complex environments and handling objections—combining sales and engineering has been a pivotal journey. I've learned so much in a short time about what it takes to enable enterprises at scale.

what drives me

I'm drawn to problems where engineering meets strategy. Whether it's designing scalable solutions for enterprise customers, building growth loops for startups, or securing critical infrastructure, I thrive when I can see the full picture: the technical implementation, the business impact, and the user experience.

My goal is to build, secure, and scale—creating products and systems that matter.
